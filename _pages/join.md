## Open Positions

The AMPERE Laboratory at the University of Notre Dame is actively recruiting motivated students and researchers to join our team. We work on exciting problems at the intersection of electric machine design, power electronics, and physics-based control — with real hardware demonstrations and strong connections to industry and national labs.

---

## PhD Students

We have **openings for PhD students** starting **Fall 2025 and Fall 2026**.

**We are looking for students with:**
- A B.S. or M.S. in Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, or a closely related field
- Strong fundamentals in electromagnetics, circuits, or control systems
- Hands-on experience with hardware (desirable but not required)
- Interest in at least one of: electric machine design, power electronics, optimization, or AI/ML applied to engineering

**What you will get:**
- Full funding (tuition + stipend) through research assistantship
- Access to state-of-the-art labs and computing resources at Notre Dame
- Mentorship and close collaboration with the PI
- Opportunities to collaborate with national labs (DOE, NASA) and industry partners
- A strong publication track record and preparation for academic or industry careers

**To apply:**
Please send an email to [ngadiyar@nd.edu](mailto:ngadiyar@nd.edu) with the subject line **"Prospective PhD Student — [Your Name]"** and include:
1. Your CV/resume
2. A brief statement (1–2 paragraphs) describing your research interests and why you want to join the AMPERE Lab
3. Unofficial transcripts
4. Links to any publications, projects, or GitHub repositories

You should also apply formally through the [Notre Dame EE PhD program](https://engineering.nd.edu/graduate-programs/graduate-admissions/) and mention Prof. Gadiyar in your application.

## Postdoctoral Researchers

Postdoc positions may be available depending on funding. If you have a strong publication record and research experience in electric machines, power electronics, or related areas, please email [ngadiyar@nd.edu](mailto:ngadiyar@nd.edu) with your CV and a short research statement.

## Undergraduate Researchers

Notre Dame undergraduate students interested in research opportunities are encouraged to reach out directly to Prof. Gadiyar at [ngadiyar@nd.edu](mailto:ngadiyar@nd.edu). We welcome students from EE, ME, CS, and related departments.
