## Graduate Students {#graduate-students}

We have **openings for multiple graduate students (MS / PhD)** starting **Fall 2026** and later.

**We are looking for students with:**
- A completed B.S. or M.S. in Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, or a closely related field.
- Hands-on experience with hardware and a passion to build things.
- Strong understanding of electric machines, power electronics, circuits, control systems, or a related area.
- Interest in at least one of: electric machine design, power electronics, optimization, or AI/ML applied to engineering.

**What you will get:**
- Full funding (tuition + stipend) through assistantships.
- Access to state-of-the-art laboratory and computing resources at Notre Dame.
- Mentorship and close collaboration with the PI.
- Opportunities to collaborate with cross-disciplinary partners from academia, industry, and national labs.
- A strong publication track record and preparation for academic or industry careers.

**To apply:**
- Please fill this google form to indicate your interest in joining our group. 

- You must apply formally through the [Notre Dame EE PhD program]([https://engineering.nd.edu/graduate-programs/graduate-admissions/](https://ee.nd.edu/graduate/)). Feel free to mention Dr. Gadiyar in your application if you like to join us.

---

## Postdoctoral Researchers {#postdoctoral-researchers}

- Multiple funded post-doc positions are available. If you have a strong publication record and hands-on research experience in electric machines, power electronics, or related areas, please fill this form and also email Prof. Gadiyar with your CV and a short research statement.
- We are also happy to host you if you are funded through post-doc fellowships / grants.
- Notre Dame also has a prestigious provost's post-doctoral fellowship. If interested, please review [this page](https://engineering.nd.edu/provosts-postdoctoral-fellowships-in-engineering-program/) and get in touch with Dr. Gadiyar. The application deadline is typically in early December.

---

## Undergraduate Researchers {#undergraduate-researchers}

- Notre Dame undergraduate students interested in research opportunities are encouraged to reach out directly to. We welcome students from EE, ME, CS, and related departments.
- Undergraduate students from other universities can be hosted under the REU program. Please review the program details [here](https://summersession.nd.edu/programs/reu/).

## Visiting Scholars {#visiting-scholars}

We are always happy to host visiting graduate students and researchers. If you are interested in spending a semester or longer at Notre Dame on research collaboration, please get in touch.
